## Overview

This skill helps founders navigate tax season efficiently. Companies that operated at any point during the previous calendar year have filing obligations. The goal is to deal with taxes quickly so you can refocus on building your business.

---

## Tax Season Process

### The Four Steps

1. **Gather data** about your business
2. **Consult** with your accountant using that data
3. **Review** the return(s) prepared by your accountant
4. **Submit** returns and pay any required tax

---

## Key Deadlines

| Filing | Due Date | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Delaware Franchise Tax + Annual Report (Corps) | March 1 | File early with tax prep |
| LLC Partnership Returns (Form 1065) | March 15 | If multi-member LLC |
| Federal Corporate Income Tax (Form 1120) | April 15 | For calendar year corps |
| Delaware LLC Tax | June 1 | $300 flat fee |

## Required Filings for Delaware Corporations

### 1. US Federal Income Tax
- Form 1120 for C corporations
- Professional preparation required

### 2. Delaware Franchise Tax + Annual Report
- Due March 1 each year
- Most Atlas companies pay minimum: **$450**
  - $400 franchise tax (Assumed Par Value method)
  - $50 annual report filing fee

### 3. State Income Tax (Maybe)
- Depends on where company has physical presence
- Consult accountant for specific obligations

### For LLCs
- Delaware LLC tax: $300 due June 1
- May need federal filings depending on structure:
  - Single-member (non-US owner): Form 5472
  - Multi-member: Form 1065 (partnership return)

## Preparing Your Data

### The Accountant's Role
Accountants are engineers of money. They don't do the underlying data entry - that's bookkeeping. They need your financial statements ready before they can prepare returns.

### What Accountants Need

#### 1. General Ledger
A list of ALL transactions for the corporation:
- Day
- Amount
- Two accounts involved
- Category
- Brief description

Can range from dozens to millions of entries depending on business activity.

#### 2. Balance Sheet
Summary of what company owns and owes at start AND end of tax year:

| Assets | Start of Year | End of Year |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Cash | $500 | $500 |
| Checking Account | $18,000 | $37,000 |
| **Total Assets** | **$27,000** | **$45,000** |

| Liabilities | Start of Year | End of Year |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| Accounts Payable | $3,600 | $2,300 |
| Unearned Income | $12,000 | $24,000 |
| **Total Liabilities** | **$20,000** | **$35,000** |
| **Shareholder Equity** | **$7,000** | **$10,000** |

📝 **Fun fact**: Shareholder equity = Total Assets - Total Liabilities (always!)

⚠️ **Note**: Balance sheet value ≠ investor valuation

#### 3. Income Statement (P&L)
Sum of revenue, expenses, and net income for the year:

| Revenue | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| SaaS Subscriptions | $75,000 |
| **Total Revenue** | **$75,000** |

| Expenses | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Software | $5,000 |
| Hosting | $10,000 |
| **Total Expenses** | **$40,000** |

| **Net Income** | **$35,000** |

### Additional Information Accountants Ask

- Who owns the company and by what percentages
- Where the company has physical operations
- Main lines of business
- Whether the corporation has depreciating assets

## Delaware Franchise Tax Details

### Information You Need

1. **Number of authorized shares** (set at incorporation)
2. **Number of shares issued** (from your cap table)
3. **Par value of shares** (set at incorporation)
4. **Gross assets of the company** (from Schedule L of Form 1120, sourced from balance sheet)

### Cost Factors for Tax Preparation

- Whether financial statements are complete
- Whether records are well-organized
- Complexity of business
- Special reporting requirements

## Tax Season Checklist

### Before Tax Season (Year-Round)
- [ ] Maintain organized bookkeeping
- [ ] Use accounting software consistently
- [ ] Keep all receipts and documentation
- [ ] Track equity issuances accurately

### January-February
- [ ] Finalize books for previous year
- [ ] Generate financial statements from bookkeeping software:
  - [ ] General ledger
  - [ ] Balance sheet
  - [ ] Income statement (P&L)
- [ ] Gather ownership information
- [ ] Identify all states with physical presence
- [ ] Connect with accountant early

### By March 1
- [ ] File Delaware franchise tax
- [ ] File Delaware annual report
- [ ] Pay franchise tax ($450 minimum for most)

### By March 15
- [ ] File LLC partnership returns (if applicable)

### By April 15
- [ ] File federal corporate income tax (Form 1120)
- [ ] Pay any federal taxes owed

### By June 1
- [ ] Pay Delaware LLC tax ($300) if applicable

## How to Help Founders

### Common Questions

1. **"What do I absolutely need to file?"**
   - Delaware franchise tax + annual report (March 1)
   - Federal corporate income tax (April 15)
   - Check with accountant about state obligations

2. **"I don't have an accountant yet!"**
   - Get one immediately
   - Stripe Atlas can connect you with startup-focused accountants
   - This is not optional for tax filings

3. **"My books aren't ready..."**
   - Prioritize getting them done NOW
   - Use bookkeeping software to generate statements
   - Consider hiring bookkeeper to catch up
   - Can't file taxes without financial statements

4. **"How much will tax prep cost?"**
   - Depends on complexity and record organization
   - Well-organized simple business: few hundred dollars
   - Complex/messy records: significantly more
   - Volume pricing through Atlas partners can help

5. **"I missed a deadline!"**
   - Contact accountant immediately
   - File as soon as possible
   - May be penalties but better late than never
   - Prevention: put deadlines in calendar now

6. **"How do I know how many shares were issued?"**
   - Check your cap table
   - Review founder stock purchase agreements
   - Atlas tool can show documents you signed
   - Remember: founders must purchase shares to finalize issuance

### Pre-Tax Season Audit

Before connecting founder with accountant, verify they have:
- [ ] Bookkeeping software set up
- [ ] All 2023 transactions recorded
- [ ] Business bank account statements available
- [ ] Credit card statements available
- [ ] Understanding of who owns what percentage

### Red Flags

- No bookkeeping in place
- Mixed personal/business finances
- Missing months of transaction records
- No idea how many shares issued
- Never heard of franchise tax

## Key Takeaways

1. **Deadlines matter**: March 1 (franchise tax), April 15 (federal income tax)
2. **Get your books ready first**: Accountants can't work without financial statements
3. **Use professionals**: Corporate tax returns require accountant involvement
4. **Delaware franchise tax is simple**: Most Atlas companies pay $450
5. **Start early**: January/February, not March
6. **Stay organized year-round**: Makes everything easier and cheaper
